# Underwriter Discovery Prereg (frozen before outreach)

Date frozen: 2026-07-02. This documents what a small outreach experiment is
allowed to conclude, before it runs. It follows the same discipline as
[ratification-prereg.md](ratification-prereg.md): decide what counts as a
reading first, so the outcome cannot be reinterpreted after the fact.

## Claim under test

The C-premise: that AI-liability underwriting and claims forensics will need
per-work-unit, tamper-evident evidence of process control over agent-produced
work — i.e. that the shape of `attention-decision.json` (see
[underwriting-evidence-inventory.md](underwriting-evidence-inventory.md)) is
something an underwriter would consume for pricing or post-loss replay.

## Method

- One-to-one email to a small number (3–5) of standalone AI-liability
  underwriting teams (MGAs / insurer programs currently writing dedicated AI
  liability cover). No mass sequences, no tracking, one follow-up at most.
- Each email asks ONE question: *what evidence of development-process
  control do you ask for today when underwriting an organization whose
  software is substantially built by coding agents — and what field is
  missing from this artifact for it to be usable?* Link to the one-page
  inventory and the two committed examples.
- No product pitch. The email offers an artifact to react to, not a tool to
  buy. There is nothing to buy.

## Reading criteria (frozen)

- **C-premise supported**: any reply that names concrete evidence
  requirements — whether or not our format satisfies them. ("We'd need X,
  you lack Y" is a strong positive reading even though it is a criticism.)
- **C-premise weakened**: a reply stating that underwriting prices on other
  bases (litigation history, model vendor, revenue class) and that
  process-control evidence is not consulted. This is a real negative reading
  and must be recorded as such, not explained away.
- **Noise (no reading)**: no replies. Cold-email base rates make silence
  uninformative. Silence does not confirm and does not refute.
- **Contaminated**: a reply that is polite interest without any concrete
  evidence requirement ("interesting, let's stay in touch"). Recorded, but
  licenses nothing.

## Stop conditions

- Do not build underwriter-facing features on the basis of one supportive
  reply. One reply licenses a second conversation, not a roadmap.
- If two or more replies land as C-premise-weakened, the "insurers as the
  structural consumer of accountability evidence" thesis is downgraded and
  the artifact reverts to its F-line (oversight/compliance) framing.
- Outcome, including null, gets recorded in this file after the window
  closes (target: 4 weeks from first send).
